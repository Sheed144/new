Drama Books


As the title implies in this section you will be introduced to some of the best Drama books the world has to offer.

Hamlet


William Shakespare

The ghost of the King of Denmark tells his son Hamlet to avenge
murder by killing the new king, Hamlet's uncle. Hamlet feigns madness,
contemplates life and death, and seeks revenge. His uncle, fearing for
his life, also devises plots to kill Hamlet. The play ends with a duel,
during which the King, Queen, Hamlet's opponent and Hamlet himself are all killed.


The Fault in Our Stars


John Green

The Fault in Our Stars by John Green tells the story of Hazel,
a young sixteen-year-old girl who struggles with thyroid cancer.
She meets Augustus and falls for him. However, her life changes
when she meets the writer she has always cherished and discovers
he is far from the person she imagined.


Othello


William Shakespare

Iago is furious about being overlooked for promotion
and plots to take revenge against his General; Othello, the Moor of Venice.
Iago manipulates Othello into believing his wife Desdemona is unfaithful, stirring Othello's jealousy.
Othello allows jealousy to consume him, murders Desdemona, and then kills himself.


To kill a Mockingbird


Harper Lee

To Kill a Mockingbird is a coming-of-age story about a girl named Scout.
Scout and her brother Jem try to understand and relate to their father,Atticus.
Atticus, who is a lawyer charged with defending a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman.


Death of a Salesman


Arthur Miller

After many years on the road as a traveling salesman,
Willy Loman realizes he has been a failure as a father and a husband.
His sons, Happy and Biff, are not successful—on his terms (being “well liked”) or any others.
His career fading, Willy escapes into dreamy reminiscences of an idealized past.
